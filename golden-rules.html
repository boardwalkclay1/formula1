The Core Rules I Trade By

These are the rules behind the Golden Formula. They are simple on purpose. The goal is to only take trades that are obvious, clean, and easy to explain to a beginner.

  1. Only trade obvious setups. If you have to squint or argue with yourself, skip it.
  2. Use two moving averages as your backbone. One fast, one slow. When they cross and price respects them, pay attention.
  3. Respect highs, lows, and even numbers. Big turns often happen at clean levels: prior highs/lows and simple even prices.
  4. Wait for a clear reversal candle. Don’t guess bottoms or tops. Let the candle show a real fight and flip.
  5. Only trade full patterns. Double bottoms, double tops, rounding bottoms, rounding tops—no half‑built shapes.
  6. Always know your stop before you enter. A simple rule: about 20% risk from your entry idea.
  7. Never chase. If price already left your even number or your zone, let it go.
  8. One clear idea at a time. Either you’re playing a bounce, a breakdown, or a breakout—not all three.
  9. Size small enough to sleep. If you can’t sleep with the position on, it’s too big.
  10. Review every trade. Win or lose, ask: did I follow the rules or did I freestyle?
