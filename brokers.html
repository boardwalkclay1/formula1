Where You Can Actually Place Trades

The Golden Formula tools are for learning and planning. To place real trades, you need a brokerage account. Here are some common places people use.

Popular Broker Choices

  • Robinhood: Simple app for stocks and options.
    Visit Robinhood
  • Webull: More charts and tools, still beginner‑friendly.
    Visit Webull
  • Chase (J.P. Morgan Investing): Connects with your Chase bank account if you use Chase.
    Visit Chase Investing
  • TD Ameritrade: Full‑featured platform with deep tools and research.
    Visit TD Ameritrade

What You Usually Need to Open an Account

  1. Basic info: Name, address, date of birth.
  2. ID: Driver’s license or other government ID.
  3. Social Security Number: For tax and identity checks.
  4. Bank info: To move money in and out.
  5. Experience questions: They may ask how much you know about stocks and options.

Simple Step‑by‑Step (Typical Flow)

  1. Go to the broker’s website or download their app.
  2. Tap or click “Open Account” or “Get Started”.
  3. Fill in your personal details and contact information.
  4. Upload or scan your ID if they ask for it.
  5. Enter your Social Security Number when requested.
  6. Answer the questions about your income, goals, and experience honestly.
  7. Connect your bank account so you can deposit money.
  8. Wait for approval (sometimes minutes, sometimes a day or two).
  9. Once approved, deposit a small amount first and practice with tiny size.
