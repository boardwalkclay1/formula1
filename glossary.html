Plain‑Language Trading Terms

This glossary is written for people who don’t know the market yet. No heavy jargon, just simple explanations.

Price

The current value of the stock. What it’s trading at right now.

High / Low

The highest and lowest prices the stock has traded at during the chosen time period.

Moving Average (MA)

A smooth line that shows the average price over a set number of candles. It helps you see the general direction instead of every tiny wiggle.

Golden Cross

When the faster moving average crosses above the slower one. It often means the trend is turning up.

Death Cross

When the faster moving average crosses below the slower one. It often means the trend is turning down.

Even Number Level

A clean, simple price like 10, 15, 20, 100. Price often reacts around these levels because they are easy for humans to see and remember.

Double Bottom

Price drops, bounces, drops again to about the same area, and then bounces harder. It looks like a “W” and can signal a possible move up.

Double Top

Price rises, pulls back, rises again to about the same area, and then falls. It looks like an “M” and can signal a possible move down.

Rounding Bottom / Top

A slow, curved turn instead of a sharp V. A rounding bottom can lead to an up move; a rounding top can lead to a down move.

Call Option

A contract that benefits if the stock goes up. In this project we just say “CALL = bet on up”.

Put Option

A contract that benefits if the stock goes down. In this project we just say “PUT = bet on down”.

Stop Loss

A planned exit point where you accept a small loss so you don’t let it turn into a big one. Here we use a simple idea: about 20% risk from your entry.
